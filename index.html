About

Research at the intersection of labor, informality, and development

Carmen Armas-Montalvo is a Ph.D. Candidate in Applied Economics at the University of Minnesota. Her work focuses on labor market dynamics, informality, and job quality in developing countries, with an emphasis on careful empirical design and applied econometric analysis.

This website is structured for a standard academic workflow: a concise profile, a research page with paper abstracts and optional draft or slide links, a teaching section, a downloadable CV, and direct contact information.
